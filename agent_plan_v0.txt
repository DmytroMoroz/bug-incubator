Itineraries often combine Tokyo (modern city temples, Tsukiji fish market) with cultural hubs Kyoto (Kiyomizu-dera, Fushimi Inari, Golden Pavilion), Nara (giant Buddha) and Hakone/Mt. Fuji (onsen spa, lake views). Avoid midsummer heat/rains; autumn brings mild days.
Flights: Round-trip Vienna–Tokyo (Narita/Haneda) ~$752 USD (≈€700) per personmomondo.com. For two, estimate ~€1,400–1,600 total. ANA/Austrian often have direct flights (~12–13h).
Sample Itinerary (≈18–20 days):
Days 1–4: Tokyo – Explore Asakusa’s Senso-ji, Meiji Shrine, Imperial Palace gardens, Shibuya, and street food (ramen, sushi)budgetyourtrip.com. Day trips to Nikko or Kamakura are optional.
Days 5–6: Hakone/Mt. Fuji – Ride the Hakone ropeway for Fuji views; stay one night at a ryokan with hot spring baths.
Days 7–12: Kyoto – Visit Kiyomizu-dera, Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion), Arashiyama bamboo forest; experience tea ceremony and Nishiki food market. Side-trips: Nara (Todai-ji temple) and Osaka (nightlife, Okonomiyaki). Kyoto alone has 17 UNESCO shrines/templesen.japantravel.com.
Days 13–14: Hiroshima & Miyajima – Train to Hiroshima (Peace Park, museum) and ferry to Miyajima island (Itsukushima Shrine).
Days 15–18: Return via Kanazawa or Takayama (traditional towns, gardens) before heading back to Tokyo for departure.
Transport: Japan Rail Pass (covers most Shinkansen/bullet trains) is highly recommended. A 14-day JR Pass costs about €515 per personjrpass.com. Local subways/buses are efficient in cities (¥250–¥500/trip, ~€2–3). For example, Tokyo⇒Kyoto by Shinkansen ¥14,000 (€95) one-way (covered by JR Pass).

Highlights: Ancient temples (Fushimi Inari’s torii gates, Golden Pavilion), tech-savvy urban scenes (robot restaurants, Akihabara), stunning nature (Mt. Fuji, Hakone lakes, Arashiyama gardens), delicious cuisine (sushi, izakaya fare, street ramen) and relaxing onsen hot springs for rest. Kyoto’s heritage temples are world-famousen.japantravel.com.
Why a Great Fit: Japan blends old and new: centuries-old shrines and tea rituals alongside cutting-edge urban culture. It offers a balanced itinerary of history (Kyoto, Nara) and nature (Fuji, forests) with globally loved foods (sushi, ramen) and plenty of downtime in hot-spring inns. Japanese hospitality and transport ease independent travel, while unique experiences (Shinkansen, sake tasting, geisha shows) make it exotic yet traveler-friendly.
