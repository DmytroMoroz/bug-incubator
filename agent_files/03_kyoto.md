## Why Kyoto

Kyoto was Japan's capital for over 1,000 years. It's the cultural heart of Japan with 17 UNESCO World Heritage sites, 2,000+ temples and shrines, traditional geisha districts, and refined cuisine. If Tokyo is Japan's future, Kyoto is its soul.

---

## Top Highlights

1. **Fushimi Inari Shrine** - Thousands of red torii gates up a mountain
2. **Kinkaku-ji (Golden Pavilion)** - Gold-covered temple on a pond
3. **Arashiyama Bamboo Grove** - Towering bamboo forest
4. **Kiyomizu-dera** - Wooden temple with city views
5. **Gion District** - Historic geisha quarter
6. **Nishiki Market** - "Kyoto's Kitchen" for food exploration

## Transport Tips

### Kyoto Bus System
- **Day pass**: ¥700 for unlimited bus rides
- **Pay when exiting** (back door enter, front door exit)
- Buses can be slow and crowded

### Better Alternatives
- **Walk**: Many areas best explored on foot
- **Train**: JR lines for Arashiyama, Fushimi, Nara
- **Taxi**: Reasonable for 2 people, especially to Kinkaku-ji

### Route Planning
- Use **Google Maps** - very accurate for Kyoto transit
- **Navitime app** also excellent

## Kyoto Etiquette Reminders

- **Gion**: Don't block geiko/maiko, don't photograph without asking
- **Temples**: Remove shoes when entering buildings, no flash photography
- **Nishiki Market**: Don't eat while walking, eat at the stall
- **General**: Queues are respected, be patient
