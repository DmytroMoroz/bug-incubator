# Budget Guide

## Price Ranges (per person)

| Category | Budget (€) | Mid (€€) | Splurge (€€€) |
|----------|------------|----------|---------------|
| **Breakfast** | €3-5 (konbini) | €8-12 (cafe) | €15-25 (hotel/Tsukiji) |
| **Lunch** | €8-12 | €15-25 | €30-50 |
| **Dinner** | €12-18 | €25-40 | €50-100+ |
| **Snacks/drinks** | €2-5 | | |

## Money-Saving Tips

1. **Convenience stores** - Onigiri, bento boxes are cheap and good
2. **Lunch specials** - Many restaurants have cheaper lunch sets
3. **Standing restaurants** - "Tachinomi" bars are cheaper
4. **Conveyor belt sushi** - Kura Sushi, Sushiro are affordable
5. **Supermarket bento** - Evening discounts (look for stickers)

# Dietary Notes

## Vegetarian/Vegan

Japan is challenging for vegetarians (dashi broth is in everything).

**Best options:**
- Shojin ryori (Buddhist temple cuisine) - fully vegetarian
- Indian restaurants (many in Tokyo)
- Kyoto tofu restaurants
- Saying: "Watashi wa bejitarian desu" (I am vegetarian)

## Allergies

| Allergy | Japanese |
|---------|----------|
| Fish/seafood | 魚介類 (gyokai-rui) |
| Shellfish | 甲殻類 (koukaku-rui) |
| Eggs | 卵 (tamago) |
| Dairy | 乳製品 (nyuu-seihin) |
| Nuts | ナッツ (nattsu) |
| Wheat/Gluten | 小麦 (komugi) |

**Tip:** Write your allergies in Japanese to show restaurant staff
